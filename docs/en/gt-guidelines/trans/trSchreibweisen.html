Spellings, Special Characters and Symbols

The text is always transcribed true to the original in Unicode format (encoding in UTF-8) of the Unicode standard valid at the time of the transcription.

Characters that are not displayed on a German keyboard are written either
  • as a Unicode hexadecimal entity
  • or as a sign.
Mixing different Unicode spellings should be avoided.

Characters are transcribed according to the respective trancription level.

  • Distinction between I and J
    The sets of black letter types usually have only one grapheme for the modern capital letters I and J.
  • Distinction between u/f, u/v and v/u
  • s-Graphemes
  • r-Graphemes
  • Ligatures
  • Umlauts
  • Abbreviation Lines
    Abbreviation lines, which can be placed as bars or wavy lines above letters, are in most cases substituents for omitted characters. The transcription is regulated in the respective levels.

  • Hyphenation
